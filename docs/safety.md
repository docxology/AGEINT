# Safety Boundary

AGEINT includes cyber, HUMINT, influence, FININT, export-control, sanctions,
historical, AI-governance, and intelligence-cycle topics. This project frames
them as lawful education, defensive analysis, historical study, governance, and
synthetic exercises. The curriculum is explicitly non-operational: avoid
instructions for unauthorized collection, evasion, exploitation, manipulation,
or targeting.

Risky motifs such as cover generation, target tracking, Shodan-style exposure review, cyber response, and ICS response are allowed only as authorized, synthetic, defensive, tabletop, or owned-lab exercises. Generated prose should preserve that boundary in every relevant module.

Economic-security motifs such as sanctions, illicit-finance typologies,
beneficial-ownership uncertainty, export controls, supplier risk, and red flags
are allowed only as compliance, policy, resilience, and due-diligence topics.
Do not turn them into instructions for laundering funds, evading sanctions,
bypassing export controls, gaming thresholds, or targeting real firms.

When adding material, write from the perspective of a curriculum designer,
analyst, defender, instructor, or governance reviewer. Prefer source
evaluation, provenance capture, tabletop inject design, policy comparison,
rubric construction, and after-action learning. Reject stepwise offensive
procedures, live infrastructure lookups, evasion advice, credential handling,
impersonation guidance, persuasion operations, or process-control actions.

Figures follow the same boundary: historical images must be public domain with
provenance, synthetic concept plates must avoid real targets and people, and
Mermaid/Python diagrams should explain concepts without operational tasking.

Generated chapters group safety-sensitive material into reader-facing
translation, governance/rights/assurance, practice-studio, and source-map
sections. Change those safety treatments in `src/manuscript_manifest/`,
`src/intelligence_content/`, or the neutral templates, not in
`output/manuscript`.
